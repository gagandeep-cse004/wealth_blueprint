üíº What are Mutual Funds & Their Types

Mutual funds pool money from multiple investors and invest in a diversified mix of assets ‚Äî equities, bonds, or money market instruments. Managed by professionals, they simplify investing for beginners and experts alike by offering diversification, liquidity, and long-term growth.

How it works ‚Äî simplified

  1. üí∞ Pooling: Investors buy fund units, pooling their money together.
  2. üìä Management: Fund managers allocate investments strategically.
  3. üìà Returns: Profits and income are distributed based on unit holdings.
  4. üíπ NAV: The Net Asset Value reflects the per-unit price, updated daily.
Remember: Mutual funds make diversification and professional management accessible ‚Äî no need to pick individual stocks.

Key Vocabulary

NAV

Net Asset Value = (Assets ‚àí Liabilities) / Outstanding Units. Reflects daily unit value.

Expense Ratio

Annual percentage covering fund management & operational costs. Lower = better returns.
